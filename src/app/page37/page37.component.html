FINE MOTOR SKILLS (Finger & Gesture Smart)
Fine Motor Skills (FMS) is the co-ordination of small muscular movements of the body in synchronization with the thought process. People with high FMS are very good at Dexterity (Finger based activities), hand-eye co-ordination and presentation with expressions. They tend to be pro-active towards kinesthetic challenges which involves action by hands. They are experienced based learners and understand better by hands-on activity.
FMS Defines Efficiency in Dexterity & Facial Expressions
  • Small & Micro muscular movements
  • Writing, Drawing, Coloring, Crafting etc.
  • Finishing touches (Finesse), Presentation
  • Chewing, Facial expression / suppression
  • All Precision based tasks
  • Cutting, Chopping, Stitching etc.
  • All Indoor games- carom, dart, pool etc.
  • Racket & Reflex based sports
CHARACTERSTICS OF PERSON HAVING HIGH FINE MOTORS SKILL
PROS
  • Can excel in Sports- Carom, Dart, Pool, Snooker,
    Spinner & Racket based sports etc.
  • Can excel in Music— Playing of Guitar, Piano,
    Table, Congo, Bongo, etc. _
  • Can excel in Fine Arts — Drawing, Painting,
    Sketching, Calligraphy, Sculpturing etc.
  • Good in Precision based Tasks &
    Non-fire Based Cooking.
CONS
  • Require mobility of fingers to pay attention
    or concentrate.
  • Constantly Tapping, Scribbling or Playing
    Rhythms on tables may cause irritation to others.
  • Fingers tend to be restless when over-thinking.
  • Although good at finger based activities but also
    require contributions from other intelligences.
REMEDIAL MEASURES IF YOUR FINE MOTOR SKILLS IS LOW
Take measures to improve the following
1) FIND A WAY TO DAILY EXERCISE FINGERS
  • Indulge into target/ racket based sports which occupies your fingers.
  • Play musical instruments where finger movements are required.
  • Develop a precision based hobby like watch repairing, handcraft, painting etc.
2) JOIN ACTING CLASSES. OBSERVE & PRACTICE DIFFERENT EXPRESSIONS
3) STOP ALL OTHER ACTIVITIES WHILE EATING AND PAY ATTENTION TO MASTICATING WHILE CHEWING
4) TAKE EXTRA CARE OF YOUR TEETH. REGULAR BRUSHING AND PERIODIC CHECK UPS
5) WORK ON YOUR PRESENTATION SKILLS. PRACTICE REPETITIVELY
6) INDULGE INTO THERAPEUTIC FINE MOTOR ACTIVITIES. Example-STACKING PENNIES,
MOVING BEANS, USING STRESS BALL, RUBBER BAND EXERCISES, ETC
